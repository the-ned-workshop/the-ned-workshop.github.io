## Article I — Name

The name of the organization shall be **The Ned Workshop** (the "Corporation"), a Colorado nonprofit corporation with its principal place of activity in Nederland, Boulder County, Colorado.

## Article II — Purpose

1. The mission of The Ned Workshop is to **create, play, make, learn—together**: to provide the Nederland community and surrounding mountain area with shared access to tools, equipment, workspace, and skill-building education.

2. The Corporation is organized exclusively for charitable and educational purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as amended (the "Code"), including but not limited to:
   1. Operating a community tool library that provides the public with access to tools, equipment, and workspace;
   2. Offering free or low-cost workshops, classes, and skill-building programs accessible to all ages and skill levels;
   3. Fostering creative, technical, and artistic expression in the local community;
   4. Promoting reuse, repair, and resourcefulness through shared infrastructure; and
   5. Conducting community outreach and partnerships, including but not limited to participation in local farmer's markets and collaboration with other community organizations.

3. No part of the net earnings of the Corporation shall inure to the benefit of, or be distributable to, its Directors, Officers, or other private persons, except that the Corporation may pay reasonable compensation for services rendered and may make payments and distributions in furtherance of the purposes set forth above.

4. The Corporation does not have members within the meaning of the Act. All corporate authority is vested in the Board of Directors.

## Article III — Definitions

1. A **"Simple Majority"** is the next integer greater than fifty percent of the total.

2. The **"Act"** means the Colorado Revised Nonprofit Corporation Act, as amended.

3. The **"Board"** means the Board of Directors of the Corporation.

## Article IV — Board of Directors

1. The Board of Directors shall serve without pay, except that Directors may be reimbursed for reasonable expenses incurred in the performance of their duties.

2. The Board of Directors shall consist of no fewer than three and no more than nine Directors, as determined from time to time by the Board.

3. Directors shall be at least eighteen (18) years of age. Directors are not required to be residents of Colorado, although Board composition should reflect a meaningful connection to the Nederland community and the Corporation's mission.

4. A majority of Directors shall be unrelated by blood, marriage, or business co-ownership, in order to maintain the Corporation's status as a public charity.

5. Directors shall serve a term of two years. Terms shall be staggered so that approximately half of the Director seats are up for election in any given year.

6. The Executive Director and Treasurer (if a non-Director is appointed to either role) are non-voting, ex officio members of the Board.

7. Vacancies that arise during the middle of a term shall be filled by appointment of the Board of Directors. A Director appointed to fill a vacancy shall serve the remainder of the unexpired term.

8. Nominees for election to the Board of Directors must state acceptance of the nomination prior to the election in which they have been nominated.

9. There is no limit on the number of consecutive terms a Director may serve.

## Article V — Nomination and Election of Directors

1. A general election of the Board of Directors shall take place at the Annual Meeting of the Board.

2. Proposed nominations for the Board of Directors shall be made by the Nominating Committee, or, if no Nominating Committee has been appointed, by any Director.

3. For the purposes of identifying qualified candidates, the Board of Directors may appoint a Nominating Committee no later than 90 days prior to the Annual Meeting.
   1. The Nominating Committee shall be chaired by a Director who is not standing for re-election.
   2. The Board shall direct the Nominating Committee as to the number of nominees to be recommended for the upcoming election.
   3. The Nominating Committee shall deliver a single slate of recommended nominees to the Board of Directors no later than 45 days prior to the Annual Meeting.

4. Final determination of the slate of nominees shall be made by the Board of Directors.

5. Directors shall be elected by the affirmative vote of a Simple Majority of the Directors then in office.

## Article VI — Resignation, Termination, and Absences of Directors

1. Resignation from the Board must be in writing and received by the Secretary or the President.

2. A Director may be removed for excess absences from the Board if he or she has three unexcused absences from Board meetings in a twelve-month period.

3. A Director may be removed, with or without cause, by a three-fourths vote of the remaining Directors at a meeting for which notice of the proposed removal has been provided to all Directors at least seven days in advance.

## Article VII — Officers

1. The Officers of the Corporation shall consist of a President, Vice President, Secretary, Treasurer, and Executive Director. Officers shall serve until new Officers have been duly elected. Any two offices may be held by the same person, except that the offices of President and Secretary may not be held by the same person.

2. Officers shall be nominated by the Board and approved by Simple Majority vote of the Board of Directors at a meeting of the Board called within a reasonable time after the general election of Directors.

3. Officers shall serve a term of one year.

4. **Responsibilities**

   a. **The President shall:**
      1. Preside at all meetings of Directors and discharge the duties of a presiding officer;
      2. Present at each Annual Meeting a report of the business of the Corporation for the preceding fiscal year; and
      3. Perform whatever other duties the Board of Directors may from time to time prescribe.

   b. **The Vice President shall:**
      1. Assume the duties of the President in case of the President's absence, resignation, or incapacitation; and
      2. Perform such other duties as the Board or President may assign.

   c. **The Secretary shall:**
      1. Serve as the custodian of the Corporation's records;
      2. Ensure accurate recording of the minutes of all Board meetings;
      3. Keep all approved minutes in a secure location; and
      4. Give required notices of meetings.

   d. **The Treasurer shall:**
      1. Maintain an accurate record of the Corporation's budget and accounts;
      2. Prepare financial reports as needed;
      3. Ensure that required tax filings are made; and
      4. Present financial reports to the Board.

   e. **The Executive Director shall:**
      1. Be the chief executive officer of the Corporation; and
      2. Have general supervision of the business of the Corporation.

5. Any Officer may be removed by the affirmative vote of a Simple Majority of the Board, with or without cause. Vacancies in any office shall be filled by the Board for the unexpired term.

## Article VIII — Committees

1. The Board may appoint standing and ad hoc committees as needed. Every committee shall have at least one Director as chairperson or co-chairperson of the committee.

2. Ad hoc committees are created at the discretion of the Board.

3. The Board may also establish advisory committees, working groups, or task forces, which may include non-Directors. Such bodies shall not have authority to bind the Corporation but may make recommendations to the Board.

## Article IX — Meetings

1. **Board Meetings**

   a. A Simple Majority of current Directors — present physically or electronically — shall constitute a quorum.

   b. In the absence of a quorum, no formal action shall be taken except to adjourn the meeting to a subsequent date.

   c. Passage of a motion requires a Simple Majority.

   d. The President does not cast a vote except in the case of a tie.

   e. An elected Director who is also serving as Interim Executive Director or Treasurer may vote, except as prohibited by Article X — Conflict of Interest.

   f. Any Director, including the President, may make a motion before the Board.

   g. Real estate owned by The Ned Workshop shall not be sold without a supermajority of 80% or more of Directors voting in favor of the sale.

   h. Any or all Directors may participate in a meeting by means of conference telephone, video conference, or any similar communications equipment by which all persons participating in the meeting can hear each other simultaneously. Such participation shall constitute presence in person at the meeting.

   i. Any action required or permitted to be taken at a Board meeting may be taken without a meeting if all Directors consent to the action in writing or by electronic transmission. Such consent shall have the same effect as a unanimous vote.

2. **Regular Board Meetings**

   a. The Board shall meet no fewer than four times per calendar year at regularly scheduled Board meetings.

   b. The Secretary shall provide the Board of Directors a draft of minutes for all Board meetings no later than five days after the meeting is adjourned.

   c. At the discretion of the Board, the Board may conduct off-site meetings for the general purpose of facilitating discussion and interpersonal interactions and relationships. No binding Board action may take place at such a meeting.

3. **Special Board Meetings**

   a. Special Board meetings may be held at any time when called for by the President or by a Simple Majority of Board Members.

   b. Special Board meetings shall be scheduled with at least three days' notice to all Directors.

   c. The agenda of a Special Board meeting shall be announced to all Directors no less than one day in advance.

   d. No business, other than the business that was set forth in the agenda of the meeting, may be transacted at the Special Board meeting.

4. **Annual Meeting**

   a. An Annual Meeting of the Board for the purposes of electing Directors and Officers, reviewing the prior year, and conducting such other business as may properly come before the meeting shall be held each year at a time and place set by the Board of Directors and announced to all Directors no less than thirty days in advance.

   b. The Annual Meeting is considered a regular Board meeting for the purposes of conducting any other business.

## Article X — Conflict of Interest

1. The Board shall adopt and maintain a written conflict-of-interest policy consistent with the requirements applicable to organizations described in Section 501(c)(3) of the Code. Each Director and Officer shall annually disclose any actual or potential conflicts of interest in writing.

2. Any Director of the Board who has a financial interest in, or conflict (or the appearance of a conflict) with, any matter pending before the Board, of such nature that it prevents or may prevent that Director from acting in an impartial manner, will offer to the Board to voluntarily excuse themself, will temporarily vacate their seat, and refrain from voting on said item.

3. The minutes of any meeting at which a conflict is disclosed shall record the disclosure, the recusal, the alternatives considered, and the basis for the Board's decision.

## Article XI — Fiscal Policies

1. The fiscal year of The Ned Workshop shall be the calendar year.

2. The Corporation shall keep correct and complete books and records of account and minutes of meetings of the Board and any committees.

3. The Board shall adopt and maintain financial policies and internal controls appropriate to the size and complexity of the Corporation, including but not limited to dual-signature or dual-approval requirements for significant disbursements, separation of duties to the extent practicable, and an annual review of financial statements.

4. The Treasurer, or such other person as the Board designates, shall ensure that the Corporation timely files all required federal and state tax returns and informational filings, including IRS Form 990 (or 990-EZ or 990-N, as applicable) and Colorado periodic reports.

## Article XII — Amendments

1. Any Director may propose an amendment to these Bylaws.

2. The text or substance of any proposed amendment shall be delivered to each Director at least fourteen days prior to the meeting at which a vote on the proposed amendment shall be conducted.

3. These Bylaws may be amended, restated, or repealed by the affirmative vote of two-thirds of the Directors then in office at any regular or special meeting of the Board for which proper notice has been given.

## Article XIII — Indemnification

1. **Scope of Indemnification.** The Ned Workshop shall indemnify each person who is or was a Director or Officer or authorized agent of The Ned Workshop, and shall pay or reimburse in advance their expenses, to the fullest extent permissible under the Colorado Revised Nonprofit Corporation Act (the "Act"). The Ned Workshop shall also indemnify each person who is or was an employee or volunteer of The Ned Workshop, and shall pay or reimburse in advance their expenses, to the same extent as Directors and Officers of The Ned Workshop. The Ned Workshop in its discretion may also purchase insurance insuring its obligations hereunder or otherwise protecting the persons intended to be protected by this Section. Any obligation that The Ned Workshop has to advance expenses under this Section shall not adversely affect any right or indemnification of any person who is or was a Director, Officer, employee, or volunteer of The Ned Workshop existing at the time of such repeal or modification. The Ned Workshop shall have the right, but shall not be obligated, to indemnify any agent of The Ned Workshop not otherwise covered by this Section to the fullest extent permissible under the Act.

2. **Savings Clause; Limitation.** If any provision of the Act or these Bylaws dealing with indemnification is invalidated by any court on any ground, then The Ned Workshop shall nevertheless indemnify each party otherwise entitled to indemnification hereunder to the fullest extent permitted by law or any applicable provision of the Act or these Bylaws that has not been invalidated. Notwithstanding any other provision of these Bylaws, The Ned Workshop shall neither indemnify any person nor advance expenses or purchase any insurance in any manner or to any extent that would jeopardize or be inconsistent with the qualification of The Ned Workshop as an organization described in § 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code, or that would result in the imposition of any liability under either § 4941 or § 4958 of the Internal Revenue Code.
